# Project B: "CampusLoop" (Circular Economy Marketplace)

## Context
At the end of every semester, dumpsters are full of good textbooks, mini-fridges, and furniture. Meanwhile, incoming students buy everything new. The Student Union wants a peer-to-peer marketplace app.

## Raw Elicitation Notes
1. Student Union President: "This needs to be exclusive. Only people with a valid .edu email address can create an account. We do not want random people from the city coming on campus to buy cheap couches."
2. Interview with Campus Police: "We are worried about safety. We want the app to suggest Safe Exchange Zones (like the library lobby) for all physical meetups. The app should force users to select a safe zone before they can chat."
3. Survey Response (Senior Student): "I want to sell my textbooks, but I hate typing in the ISBN and Title. Can I just scan the barcode with my phone camera? Also, I want to be able to auction items, not just set a fixed price."
4. University Legal Team: "The university cannot handle any money. We are not a bank. The app cannot process payments. Students have to pay each other via Venmo or cash. But the Student Union President said they want to take a 2% transaction fee to fund club activities. We need to figure that out."
